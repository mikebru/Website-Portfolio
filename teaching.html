Teaching Portfolio

As an Instructional Assistant Professor at Texas A&M University's College of Performance, Visualization and Fine Art, I teach a variety of courses focused on interactive design, digital media, and creative technology. My teaching philosophy emphasizes hands-on learning, creative problem-solving, and the integration of technical skills with artistic expression.

VIST 110

Design Foundations

An introduction to the fundamental principles of design, including composition, color theory, typography, and visual hierarchy. Students develop critical thinking and problem-solving skills through a series of hands-on projects that explore both traditional and digital media.

Key Topics

Design Principles Color Theory Typography Composition Visual Communication

VIST 210

Interactive Design

This course explores the principles and practices of designing interactive experiences. Students learn to create engaging user interfaces and interactive systems through a combination of theoretical concepts and practical applications, with a focus on user-centered design methodologies.

Key Topics

UI/UX Design Prototyping User Testing Interaction Models Web Design

VIST 289

Special Topics in Visualization

A project-based course that explores emerging technologies and specialized topics in visualization. Content varies by semester, covering areas such as virtual reality, real-time graphics, interactive installations, and experimental media. Students develop technical skills while creating innovative visual experiences.

Key Topics

Emerging Technologies TouchDesigner VR/AR Interactive Media Creative Coding

VIST 205

Digital Image

This course covers the fundamentals of digital image creation, manipulation, and optimization. Students learn industry-standard software and techniques for creating and editing digital imagery, with an emphasis on both technical proficiency and creative expression.

Key Topics

Adobe Photoshop Digital Painting Image Composition Photo Manipulation Digital Asset Creation
